Shiro

Description

Shiro is a traditional Ethiopian stew made from powdered chickpeas or broad beans, often seasoned with berbere spice blend and niter kibbeh (spiced clarified butter). It is a staple dish in Ethiopian cuisine, known for its rich, hearty flavor and smooth, thick texture. Shiro is typically served with injera, a sourdough flatbread, and is enjoyed as a comforting and satisfying meal, especially during fasting periods when meat is not consumed.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup shiro powder (chickpea or broad bean flour)
  • 2 tbsp niter kibbeh (spiced clarified butter)
  • 1 tbsp berbere spice blend
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 inch fresh ginger, minced
  • 4 cups water or vegetable broth
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Heat niter kibbeh in a large pot over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and cook until softened, about 5-7 minutes.
  2. Add the minced garlic and ginger to the pot and cook for another 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
  3. Stir in the berbere spice blend and cook for another minute to toast the spices.
  4. Gradually whisk in the shiro powder, ensuring there are no lumps, and cook for 1-2 minutes to allow the flour to toast slightly.
  5. Slowly pour in the water or vegetable broth while stirring continuously to prevent lumps from forming. Bring the mixture to a simmer.
  6. Reduce the heat to low and let it simmer gently for 20-30 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the shiro is thickened and flavorful. Adjust seasoning with salt as needed.
  7. Serve hot with injera or your choice of bread.
