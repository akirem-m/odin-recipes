Tibs

Description

Tibs is a popular Ethiopian stir-fry featuring tender cubes of meat (typically beef, lamb, or chicken) served in a rich, aromatic sauce made from niter kibbeh (spiced clarified butter), berbere spice blend, onions, garlic, and ginger. The dish is traditionally cooked by slowly caramelizing the onion base, searing the meat at high heat to develop a crust, and then combining them with a splash of lemon juice before serving with injera, rice, or pasta.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb beef, lamb, or chicken, cut into cubes
  • 1 large onion, sliced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 inch fresh ginger, minced
  • 2 tbsp niter kibbeh (spiced clarified butter)
  • 2 tbsp berbere spice blend
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice

Instructions

  1. Heat niter kibbeh in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the sliced onions and cook until caramelized, about 10-15 minutes.
  2. Add the minced garlic and ginger to the skillet and cook for another 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
  3. Increase the heat to high and add the cubed meat to the skillet. Sear the meat on all sides until browned, about 5-7 minutes.
  4. Sprinkle the berbere spice blend over the meat and stir to coat evenly. Cook for another 2-3 minutes to allow the spices to toast.
  5. Reduce the heat to medium-low, cover the skillet, and let it simmer for 20-30 minutes until the meat is tender and cooked through.
  6. Stir in the lemon juice and adjust seasoning with salt if needed. Serve hot with injera, rice, or pasta.
