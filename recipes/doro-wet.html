Doro-wet

Description

Doro-wet is a traditional Ethiopian stew made with chicken, hard-boiled eggs, and a rich, spicy sauce. The dish is typically prepared by marinating the chicken in a blend of spices, including berbere (a fiery chili powder), garlic, ginger, and niter kibbeh (spiced clarified butter). The marinated chicken is then simmered in a tomato-based sauce until tender, and hard-boiled eggs are added towards the end of cooking to absorb the flavors. Doro-wet is often served with injera, a sourdough flatbread, making it a hearty and flavorful meal that is enjoyed during special occasions and celebrations in Ethiopian cuisine.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb chicken pieces (bone-in or boneless)
  • 4 hard-boiled eggs
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 inch fresh ginger, minced
  • 2 tbsp niter kibbeh (spiced clarified butter)
  • 2 tbsp berbere spice blend
  • 1 can (14 oz) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, combine the chicken pieces with berbere spice blend, minced garlic, ginger, and niter kibbeh. Mix well to coat the chicken evenly and let it marinate for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Heat niter kibbeh in a large pot over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and cook until softened and caramelized, about 10-15 minutes.
  3. Add the marinated chicken to the pot and sear on all sides until browned, about 5-7 minutes.
  4. Stir in the crushed tomatoes and chicken broth. Bring the mixture to a simmer, then reduce the heat to low. Cover the pot and let it cook for 30-40 minutes, or until the chicken is tender and cooked through.
  5. Gently add the hard-boiled eggs to the stew and let them simmer for an additional 10 minutes to absorb the flavors. Adjust seasoning with salt as needed.
  6. Serve hot with injera or your choice of bread.
